# Morocco Law 25-06 — SDOQ Framework

## Full Title

**Loi n° 25-06 relative aux signes distinctifs d'origine et de qualité des denrées alimentaires et des produits agricoles et halieutiques**

*(Law No. 25-06 on distinctive signs of origin and quality for foodstuffs and agricultural and fishery products)*

Published in the Official Bulletin of the Kingdom of Morocco. Implementing decrees were issued subsequently, establishing the certification procedures, the structure of product specifications (cahiers des charges), and the roles of the governing bodies.

---

## The Three SDOQ Signs

Law 25-06 establishes three distinctive signs (signes distinctifs d'origine et de qualité — SDOQ) for Moroccan food and agricultural products:

### AOP — Appellation d'Origine Protégée

A product may bear the AOP sign if:
- Its production, processing, and preparation **all take place** within the defined geographical zone.
- Its quality and essential characteristics are **exclusively or essentially** due to the geographical environment of that zone, including natural factors (soil, climate, topography) and human factors (local knowledge, traditions).
- The geographical delimitation is precisely defined in the cahier des charges and validated by MAPMDREF.

AOP is the most restrictive designation. A single step of the production chain occurring outside the zone disqualifies the product. This makes GPS tracking of farms (Step 3 of the certification workflow) critical for AOP compliance.

### IGP — Indication Géographique Protégée

A product may bear the IGP sign if:
- **At least one** of the production, processing, or preparation steps takes place within the defined geographical zone.
- The product possesses a quality, reputation, or other characteristic that is attributable to that geographical origin.

IGP is less restrictive than AOP: a product could, for example, be grown in the designated region but processed elsewhere (or vice versa), provided the product's link to the region can be demonstrated.

### Label Agricole (LA)

A product may bear the Label Agricole if:
- It has **a set of specific characteristics** that give it a level of quality higher than comparable standard commercial products.
- The quality advantage is defined in a formal specification and is verifiable through defined analytical and/or organoleptic criteria.

Label Agricole is **not geographically restricted** — any Moroccan producer meeting the specification may apply, regardless of region. This distinguishes it from AOP and IGP.

---

## Key Differences: AOP vs. IGP

| Criterion | AOP | IGP |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Steps in the zone | All steps (production, processing, preparation) | At least one step |
| Link to terroir | Essential and exclusive | Attributable (may include reputation) |
| Traceability strictness | Highest — GPS coordinates validated at all steps | Moderate — at least one step documented |
| Example | Huile d'Argan (Arganeraie) | Safran de Taliouine |

## Governing Bodies

### MAPMDREF

**Ministère de l'Agriculture, de la Pêche Maritime, du Développement Rural et des Eaux et Forêts**

The central ministry with jurisdiction over Law 25-06. MAPMDREF is responsible for:
- Approving or rejecting applications for SDOQ registration.
- Overseeing the development and validation of product specifications (cahiers des charges).
- Publishing the national register of SDOQ products.
- Coordinating with ONSSA and EACCE on compliance and export matters.

On the Terroir.ma platform, MAPMDREF is the **data controller** (see `docs/morocco/cndp-compliance.md`). The `super-admin` role acts on behalf of MAPMDREF for cooperative verification and overrides.

### ONSSA

**Office National de la Sécurité Sanitaire des Produits Alimentaires**

ONSSA handles food safety enforcement across Morocco, including:
- Accrediting laboratories authorised to perform SDOQ-required analyses.
- Setting and enforcing analytical standards for food safety parameters.
- Conducting market surveillance to detect fraudulent SDOQ labelling.

On the platform, the `labId` registered against each `LabTestSubmission` must match an ONSSA-accredited laboratory. The accredited lab register is maintained as a reference table in the system, updated by `super-admin` when ONSSA publishes changes.

### EACCE

**Etablissement Autonome de Contrôle et de Coordination des Exportations**

EACCE oversees the export of Moroccan agricultural products, including:
- Issuing export clearance certificates for SDOQ products.
- Validating HS codes and destination declarations.
- Coordinating with Moroccan customs (ADII — Administration des Douanes et Impôts Indirects) for border control.

On the platform, the `customs-agent` role represents EACCE officials who validate `ExportDocument` records before export is cleared.

## Product Specifications (Cahiers des Charges)

Each SDOQ product is governed by a **cahier des charges** — a formal specification document that defines:

1. **Product description**: name, definition, principal characteristics.
2. **Geographical delimitation**: precise boundaries of the production zone (commune-level, GPS polygon, or hydrological boundary).
3. **Production methods**: authorised varieties/breeds, cultivation practices, harvest methods, processing conditions.
4. **Labelling requirements**: mandatory information, logo usage.
5. **Control and verification**: analytical parameters, inspection frequency, accredited bodies.
6. **History and tradition**: evidence of the product's link to the region or quality tradition.

### Mapping to the Platform

The cahier des charges maps to the `ProductSpecification` entity in the product module. The analytical parameters defined in each cahier des charges are translated into the structured entries in `lab-test-parameters.json`, which the platform uses at runtime to evaluate whether a batch's lab results comply (see `docs/domain/lab-parameters.md`).

The `geographicZone` field of `ProductSpecification` stores the human-readable zone description. The platform validates farm GPS coordinates against the zone at harvest logging time (Step 3).

## Platform Implementation of the 12-Step Certification Chain

Law 25-06 does not prescribe a specific number of steps, but its implementing decrees establish a sequence of: application → verification of eligibility → product specification compliance check → inspection → decision. The Terroir.ma platform formalises this into 12 discrete, event-driven steps:

| Law 25-06 Requirement | Platform Implementation |
|----------------------|------------------------|
| Cooperative must be legally registered | Step 1: `cooperative.registration.submitted` + super-admin verification |
| Product must have an approved cahier des charges | Step 2: `product.registered` linked to `ProductSpecification` |
| Traceability of production to geographic zone | Steps 3–4: GPS-validated harvest logging and batch creation |
| Analytical verification by accredited lab | Steps 5–7: ONSSA-accredited lab sample submission and results |
| Formal certification request | Step 8: `certification.requested` |
| On-site inspection by the certification body | Steps 9–10: Inspection scheduling and report filing |
| Decision by competent authority | Step 11: `certification.decision.granted/denied` |
| Marking and traceability | Step 12: QR code generation encoding the certification number |

The platform enforces state machine transitions so that no step can be skipped. For example, a certification cannot reach `granted` status without a completed inspection report, and an inspection cannot be filed without first being scheduled.

## Export Clearance

For SDOQ products destined for export, Law 25-06 requires coordination with EACCE. The platform implements this through the `ExportDocument` entity:

1. A `cooperative-admin` requests an export clearance document, specifying:
   - The certified batch (`certificationId`)
   - The applicable **HS code** (Harmonised System tariff code) for the product
   - The destination country (ISO 3166-1 alpha-2)

2. The `export.document.requested` Kafka event is emitted and logged to the audit trail.

3. A `customs-agent` (EACCE representative) reviews the document on the platform and either validates or rejects it.

4. On validation, the `export.document.validated` event is emitted and the cooperative admin is notified.

**Common HS Codes for SDOQ products:**

| Product | HS Code |
|---------|---------|
| Argan oil | 1515.90 |
| Saffron | 0910.20 |
| Olive oil | 1509.10 |
| Honey | 0409.00 |
| Dates (Medjoul) | 0804.10 |
| Rose products (essential oil) | 3301.29 |

Customs-agents validate that the HS code declared matches the certified product type. Mismatches are grounds for document rejection.
